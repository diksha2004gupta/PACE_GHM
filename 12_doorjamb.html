The Limestone Door Jamb

Origins of the Door Jamb

View the limestone door jamb from the necropolis of Saqqara, inscribed with the name of Niankhkhnum, the Superintendent of the Hairdressers of the Palace. Ancient Egyptians believed that speaking the name of the dead gave them life again.

🐦 Ibi the Ibis

"This stone once stood at the entrance to an ancient tomb. Inscribed on it is the name Niankhkhnum — Superintendent of the Hairdressers of the Palace. Ancient Egyptians believed that speaking the name of the dead gave them life again!
